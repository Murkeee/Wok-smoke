Raspberry Pi

Raspberry Pi’s, Arduninos, Makey Makeys, etc. Are small computing devices that can come in different forms. These devices are either made up of a circuit board, microcontroller or a minicomputer. Out of these the minicomputer variant is the best. As microcontrollers have far fewer available resources compared to minicomputers. But which device you want may vary for what the intended function is. As of now the best minicomputer there is, is the Intel NUC and the runner up being the UDOO BOLT. However, both options are very pricey. Intel NUC is essentially a whole desktop computer in a mini form factor depending on if you’re going to get the prebuilt Intel NUC minicomputer or just the Intel NUC board. A bare Intel NUC board can go from upwards of $330 USD, and a prebuilt board can be $1,029 USD and over. While Intel NUC are the best minicomputers out there, you don’t necessarily need it if a Raspberry Pi 4 valued at around $90 USD can be used instead. Minicomputers such as Raspberry Pi’s can do a lot of things. As of now a Raspberry Pi can essentially do many things that a normal desktop computer can normally do. Like a desktop computer it of course needs to be plugged into a monitor of sorts and needs a keyboard and mouse to function. Raspberry Pi’s can also be used to learn coding/programming and be used in many projects. Projects such as replacing your desktop computer, emulating a Gameboy, creating an AI or DIY smart assistant (Amazon Echo), controlling a robot, hooking it up to a monitor to display computer hardware status or to create a smart tv, an intruder alarm with the use of a laser sensor, a cheaper network-attached-storage device, as well as something our team is already currently looking into a Smart Mirror. There are many more prospects that a minicomputer can do. Essentially every electronic device or gadget can be made or improved with a small computing device. Theoretically you could automate almost any object or process with a computing device.

In the future new and better computing devices will come out and unlock more possibilities. For example, the upcoming Raspberry Pi 5. Computing devices such as Raspberry Pi’s are used in the Internet of Things (IoT) industry and serve as a good replacement and solution to data centers and edge computing. Edge computing is a viable solution to cloud storage. As the Raspberry Pi’s run the process locally compared to the AI process of the norm of data centers, thus reducing latency. Also due to the fact the data center isn’t run by cloud-based AI, this also resolves “many problems and concerns” that AI-run systems have (“The Future of Single Board Computers and Artificial Intelligence”, 1 Aug 2019). However, our current computing devices aren’t powerful and fast enough to efficiently run these processes. The Raspberry Pi 4 “is a potential candidate for edge-computing applications”. However, it does not locally come with an “AI co-processor, so all AI algorithms must run on the CPU” (“The Future of Single Board Computers and Artificial Intelligence”, 1 Aug 2019). In the future though, many single board computers and other computing devices could come embedded with AI or at least a “minimum level of intelligence” (“The Future of Single Board Computers and Artificial Intelligence”, 1 Aug 2019). Furthermore, with the slow global adaptation of 5G, and how it's driving the future of the Internet of Things and edge computing. With major strides in improvement of single board computers/minicomputers, 5G adoption and edge computing. Combining all of these could essentially move and improve the Internet of Things. Technological fields such as AR and VR technology could be dominated by single board computers with devices such as smart glasses and virtual reality headsets. Other fields such as robotics/drones and telemedicine will be widely affected by the improvement and adoption of 5G, edge computing and computing devices. With existing and new computing devices already out there and being made. Many physical objects in the real world could and may as well eventually be connected to the internet. Which is essentially the process of the Internet of Things. For example, simple items in your household that wouldn’t normally have any outside functionality apart from their primary purpose could eventually be linked to the internet. Objects such as one’s coffee machine or even their shower could be linked to the network. Other technological advancements in robotics and other Internet of thing related fields further push the capabilities of computing devices. Also, the components of these small computing devices further improve and also unlock more potential from them. As these small computing devices get better CPUs and RAM further pushing performance, better connection through Bluetooth or WI-FI, and storage either getting bigger or faster in terms of reading and writing speeds. In addition to, the integration of better ports such as the new standard of USB Type-C or USB 3.0. These newer and better components further develop the proficiency and power these devices hold.

Clouds, services, servers

Cloud computing is used every day around the world in many aspects of IT and daily life so much so that most people don’t even realise it. Cloud computing are services that are delivered over the internet such as storing data and information, software usage, data analytics, databases and many more. Companies which require IT infrastructure such as data centres can instead rent access to these through cloud service providers rather than having to purchase and maintain their own infrastructure. Many services that you currently use are also cloud services such as Google’s Gmail and Google Drive which both store data and information and on which the user can opt to pay for access to more storage space, Dropbox, Office365, etc. These days cloud computing has advanced so widely that you can even play modern video games on your phone by streaming the game from your PC from anywhere at any time using software such as Steam link. A major player in the cloud computing space is Amazon web services, they provide cloud services for many different categories including Advertising and Marketing, Financial services, Media and entertainment, Data analytics, machine learning, Applications and storage and gaming. Amazon Web Services or AWS, Google Cloud and Microsoft Azure are 3 services that are largely considered state of the art when it comes to cloud computing. AWS is the most popular of the 3 services and holds the highest market share due to its large and highly competitive offers with many different available services for every need and use. Google cloud is highly popular for its machine learning capabilities and is also well known for their expertise in open source and last but not least, Azure offers safe ‘tried-and-true’ services and is often the go to service for experts and professionals with long standing experience in cloud computing.


While cloud computing is still in its infancy stage it’s potential for growth is exceeding and some of advancements that we will see in the near future will allow for even more rapid growth of the adoption of cloud computing. The future of cloud computing holds many promising innovations some of the expected improvements in the near future include low latency streaming over cloud, faster and better serverless computing and better availability of quantum computing over the cloud. The advancements that make these innovations in cloud computing possible include the improvement of computer processing speeds and technologies such as already seen in recent years and the improvement of the recent invention of quantum computers which utilise quantum physics to create computers that can be faster and can computer more at a time than classical computers. Furthermore, the recent events of Covid-19 has forced many businesses and enterprises to adopt cloud-based solutions to survive and sustain themselves further accelerating the growth and adoption of cloud computing in personal and business-based environments. However, cloud computing does have its downsides; there are many risks associated with cloud computing especially for those who use it to store highly sensitive data and information as they are vulnerable to data loss and theft, data leakage, hijackings and DDOS all of which can compromise the integrity of the data stored on the servers of cloud services, therefore it is necessary to be constantly improving and innovating on the security and the recovery and restoration of data and services and even the hardware used to store the data in data-centres.


According to Gartner, Cloud services are expected to grow by 21.7% by the end of 2022 and predicts that 45% of enterprise IT spending will be on cloud computing, this highlights the importance of cloud services for the future of IT and businesses and its rapid growth surely means that IT experts and professionals will have to adapt to their usage, staying up to date with information and news about cloud computing and new innovations. While this technology is great for many businesses with this rapid growth, it’s possible that many jobs will be made unnecessary especially those that maintain and develop servers ‘in-house’ for individual clients, businesses, and companies but they won’t be entirely removed as it’s hard to imagine that cloud computing will entirely eliminate the need for local networks and on-site data storage solutions which are currently still the most dominant means of computing. Clearly the people that will be most affected are those that work in the IT industry as well as business owners and companies as the widespread adoption of cloud computing will reduce the cost of setup and maintenance for individuals, businesses and companies which will allow for greater innovation in other industries and areas by helping to reduce the factor of cost in projects and therefore allowing assets to be allocated elsewhere to improve the quality of products and services in the short term and long term.


Access to these cloud services allows smaller companies and individuals to produce better products and services while reducing the costs of maintenance and upkeep and eliminating the significant cost of setting up the necessary infrastructure in the first place.

Language processing

Chatterbots are applications that consists of Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing (referred to as NLP) designed to breakdown what end user inputs and directs them towards the outcome that they want. Often, they will analyze the request and identify context and intent before composing a reply. Natural Language Processing, according to encora.com, “is a form of AI that gives machines the ability to not just read, but to understand and interpret human language. With NLP, machines can make sense of written or spoken text and perform tasks including speech recognition, sentiment analysis, and automatic text summarization.” (Encora, 2021.). Currently, the recent stage in development of chatterbots and NLP can be summarized and conceptualized within Google’s LaMDA which stands for “Language Model for Dialogue Applications.” which demonstrated its complexity at Google I/O 2021 which reflected its ability to create intriguing conversations in full lengths with randomized topics, this is known as open-domain dialogue. Chatbots and NGLs have been used extensively to guide end users in various language-based tasks; summarizing documents, translating them into other languages or answering specific question related to information-seeking. Open domain dialogue was seen as complicated due to the open-ended challenges and the ever-growing requirement to be flexible in application, this also included those responses made by the program had to justifiable under the given context. However, Google LaMDA’s recent developments in 2021 has paved the way to safe, grounded, high-quality dialogue applications (Google AI Blog, 2022). Google’s CEO Sundar Pichai displayed a demo of LaMDA impersonating Pluto and a paper plane as two different conversations. In order to create meaning conversations with its users, it used its training data which consisted of learned concepts and a dataset of SentencePiece tokens to generate a multitude of responses that could accomplish its targets. It demonstrated the aforementioned qualities: sensibleness, specifity, interestingness, and factuality (Romero, 2021). It has unlocked new avenues for comprehending benefits and risks of open-ended dialog agents with the set AI principles (Google AI Blog, 2022). This indicates what chatbots are now capable of which is a wide variety of tasks with some human guidance or assistance.


Currently what can be done with chatbots is automation and handling of simple tasks and providing support to end users whether its internal or external. Due to the impact of Covid in 2020 and 2021, there has been an increase in uses chatbots and as such, more possibilities are becoming real in coming years. “Economic competitive pressure has driven companies to rely on Ai to automate decision making” (Landbot.io, 2020) and with functions such as speech to text, language recognition and translation, speaker recognition, content moderation, text analytics and much more; most industries could involve themselves with the applications of chatterbots. Customer service and intent recommendation could see chatterbots being used in online shopping, marketing and even customer support, involving itself through all customer touchpoints. There has also been indication that chatterbots could be used for mental health services such as aiding those individuals who are reluctant due to stigmatization or even fear; currently it’s been used for training, therapy and screening. (Abd-alrazaq et al., 2019). Some emerging trends that involve NLP have allowed Chatbots to grow, these include: AI chatbot apps, Virtual assistances, Customer service and analytics. Google’s LaMDA was built upon other language models like Google’s BERT, Meena and GPT-3, all based on Transformer which is a neural network architecture designed and development by Google in 2017 which has allowed for such developments to reach the current state of advancement. Usage of tokenizers which is essentially is words broken down into subwords and then complied as packages with IDs to be scanned with a look up table has also allowed for the growth of NPL and chatterbots such as SentencePiece and WordPiece.


The potential impact of the recent development for chatterbots and NLP has created a market and foundation for more extensive and complex usage. With many transnational corporations implementing chatbots for customer and business support such as Ebay, LinkedIn and Starbucks, users are in constant demand for round-the-clock service; with “nearly 40% of internet users worldwide prefer interacting with chatbots than virtual agents, chatbots will likely increase in popularity moving forward.” (Yuen, 2022). Google’s LaMDA has indicted what the future of chatbots could become and how involved they could be with our daily lives. We will likely see more widespread usage of chatbots to handle basic tasks and queries without the guidance of a human to affirm decisions made. Since there numerous platforms each with various contexts and data, specific chatbots could be built to help in any setting from retailers to banking and finance to entertainment and travel to medical emergencies and healthcare. This growth would create jobs designed to regulate, maintain and update chatbots and report on its feedback and gathered data. However, this would also mean the end of smaller jobs such as call center jobs and administration. Not that those particular kinds of jobs would be completely ruled out but there would be overall less reliance towards them since they would have usually handled tasks that could possibly be automatized. Yet, chatbots are not the entire solution at the moment; current models are unable to carry all queries or adjust to answer specifically and most are not as fast as Google’s LaMDA and could vary in implementation and pre-training. It is also expected that chatterbots will not remove humans from the equation but rather assist them since human skills such as adaptability and creative are far beyond the capacity of current models without being difficult to maintain as LaMDA.
